About

I'm an Electrical and Computer Engineering undergraduate at the University of Washington (GPA 3.93), targeting a BSMS with a focus on analog IC and mixed-signal design. I'm currently designing a 5T-OTA-based CMOS operational amplifier in Cadence GPDK045 (45nm) using Cadence Virtuoso (schematic and layout) and ADE Assembler, sizing devices from first-principles small-signal analysis and validating with AC, DC, and transient sweeps in ADE Explorer.

On the systems side, I lead the Husky Robotics drone subteam, where I am designing a custom STM32H743-based flight controller in Altium from schematic to PCB - integrating current sensing, USB-UART debug, and multi-protocol I/O (UART, CAN, SPI, I2C, USB, Ethernet) - alongside two supporting boards (ESC and PDB). I also serve as an Electrical Systems Engineer on the Engineering Horizons Mars Rover team. I enjoy work that spans the full hardware stack: hand-sized transistors, embedded firmware, and the empirical tests that close the loop.

Outside of school I'm interested in high-speed PCB layout, signal integrity, and power-distribution networks - always happy to connect with people working on analog, mixed-signal, and semiconductor design.

Projects

5T-OTA-Based CMOS Operational Amplifier

Cadence Virtuoso (Schematic & Layout) · ADE Assembler · GPDK045 (45nm)

Designed and optimized a CMOS operational amplifier built around a 5-transistor OTA input core in Cadence GPDK045 (45nm). Achieved 13.7 dB DC gain, 629 MHz 3-dB bandwidth, 3.05 GHz gain-bandwidth product, and 0.889 V output swing on a 1 V supply. Hand-sized the differential pair, current-mirror load, tail current source, and output stage from first-principles gm/ro analysis; ran AC, DC, and transient analyses in ADE Explorer; used ADE Assembler (Brent-Powell optimizer) for multi-objective sizing under all-saturation constraints.

Adjustable AC-DC Boost Converter

Breadboarded · LTspice · Feedback Control

Designed, fully simulated in LTspice, and breadboarded an AC-DC converter that boosts ±7.5 VAC line input to a continuously adjustable +10–20 VDC output (0–1 mA load) with <100 mV output ripple. Topology: full-wave bridge rectifier + LC filter → 2N7000 boost converter clocked by an NE555 at ~100 kHz → op-amp feedback regulator with a 1N4744 zener reference. Validated in simulation with parametric load sweeps and .meas-based automated measurement, then built and verified on hardware.
